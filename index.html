Once upon a Tiger

A child you were, fearful of the darkness
And of forests sprouting in your stomach
If you swallowed the green apple seeds
Your world appeared tiny and unassuming
And yet you couldn’t see over it

No better way had you to spend the days
Than to plot the rise and fall of worlds imagined
To scale the mountains that lay in the folds of your thin fingers
To compose a sonnet a sonata a sculpture of David
And finding that satisfactory,
Melt into a puddle of repose

Stumbling out of the closet you came,
Eyes full of the ghosts of forgotten ideas
Ears full of the crashing waves and the silence.

You fell into a Tiger black and golden
Who bowed down upon the sand
And greeted you in the voice of unsung lullabies
Right there on the beach of your bedroom floor
Among the laughing gulls, and the man o’ war

He replaced your fears with amity
Licked a tear away shed over an apple seed
It could have been a tree given time
A very small singular forest

It’s ok said the Tiger
It would have been lonely

Anna

The boy around the corner thinks my name is Miss
He lost his money buying cups for his lemonade stand
But breathtaking is his desire to comprehend the universe
He collects tadpoles in the shadows of the pond and traces patterns in the stars
He reads and adds numbers and plays the cello
Yet he cannot remember where he left his nice leather shoes
He cannot explain the stock market or the changing seasons
And he cannot sleep at night for dreaming

As little girls walk to school each morning side by merry side
They don’t imagine the unfathomable pairings
Delivered in an impenetrable box by the grey-faced future
Combinations and permutations on a common theme
They hum it each day on their merry promenade together
A prayer said at night, but they’re not sure if she’s listening
Or if anyone gives a damn at all
About little girls walking to school

They keep it out of their homes for the children fear the void
But I welcomed it in along with a breeze that smells of jasmine
The permutation that stopped at my front door
Not caring if I was in any way present
Or that I was busy forgetting the last twelve years of my life
Because I no longer hum or promenade or pray
I do not find tadpoles in the pools or patterns in the stars
And my name is Anna

The River

On a quiet raft of wood you float,
Atop the restful water
Atop the rocks beneath the water
Atop the world.

You stand straight and know no haste,
For pace is irrelevant.
It redeems watches and church bells and falling grains of sand
And now those things are absent,
Lost, beneath the water
With the rocks and the world.

Straight you stand, a sovereign.
Straight as a melancholy birch
Brittle and white.
Bark peeling for the children come Autumn
To tear you apart.

Straight as the line between this place and Proxima Centauri
Four years it would take to traverse that line if you were light
But you are not light
Or are you?
Most likely it is no longer relevant.

You see it up ahead,
It wavers.
How far you cannot ascertain by eye
Though if you wanted you could be there in the next instant.

Or perhaps you will wait.
Countlessly infinite centuries it could be.
But what are centuries?
Surely the water doesn’t know and yet it takes you through them all the same.

Christmas in Oxford

The winds of December blow grey and remote
They stiffen the soul and pinch at the throat
I pass frigid midnights immune to sleep’s pull
For days grow too empty and my mind grows too full

Cobblestoned side streets choke down inky dark
Don’t surrender come morning to the sun’s sanguine spark
In jewel frosted windows lights flicker and shine
Yet somehow the lights cease to flicker in mine

When the barren brass tones of St. Aldates begin
And the notes fail to kindle the furnace within
When the trains leave for London and soulless, return
When dear friends depart and sage conclaves adjourn

I perch by the fire on a half-conceived notion
That narration can allay a broken emotion
Can upbraid convention and audit the mind
To draw with sensation what can’t be defined

The words rise like heroes, the equations add up
The steam rises slowly from an almost-full cup
The pen glides through evenings without verdict or pause
It is governed by moonlight and symphonic laws

Its beats pierce the paper, its notes singe the air
Yet with no one to listen and no one to share
How beautiful the breaking of sunlight on the pavement
How perilous the balance between love and loneliness

Here

Ripples of clouds ushered in the morning
With the cheer of a laden hearse
And finding you were still not here
Swelled irritably into rain

In the hall a sullen floorboard creaked a crooked greeting
But that was not you nor your greeting
Just the floorboard pretending.

There is a particular pungency of parting
A conspiracy of mold spores
Empty bottles, laden sinks, and disappointment

The lights of this dark apartment seem dimmer
Voltage and resistivity and light quanta
Jettisoning themselves through space with lessened vigor
To fall in a peculiar sort of slump in the eye

The couch contains an abstraction
A sag of springs and deflation of purpose
That was not there
When you were

Mission to Mars

In the News

Click to see news, stories, and reports in which Laurel is prominently featured.

I want to see humans go to Mars in my lifetime. Such an endeavor would promote tremendous advances in health, science, technology, and the human spirit. Therefore, when the opportunity was presented in 2013 to apply for a program that aimed to send people to Mars in the next decade or two, I signed up to be among the first on Mars. Now I am a Mars-One round three candidate- an exciting opportunity to meet a passionate group of people from all across the world, and promote scientific learning.

The actual mission is still many years out- at very least a decade- but the coming years will see several unmanned missions aimed specifically at settlement, and a critical testing of technologies needed to sustain human life on such an inhospitable place. It will also focus on selecting candidates and developing proper means of training them so that we will have a group of highly capable, dedicated individuals when the time comes. Of course there is the potential that this mission might not succeed in developing a safe way for humans to travel and live on Mars, but so far this mission has already succeeded in spreading a tremendous amount awareness and interest in human space exploration, and done a good deal of research and planning that will contribute to any attempts in the future. I am very excited to contribute to this project in the coming years.

For even more information, please see my blog where I explain even more about the mission and my role in it. I also answer several frequently asked questions such as “How would you get water?” and “Would you have the ability to communicate?” and include some original artwork. If you would like your question answered in a post, there is a separate form on the blog to do that. If you are a teacher who would like me to visit or Skype into your classroom to talk about space exploration and humans going to Mars, I am very happy to do so. Please fill out a contact form and I will be in touch with you shortly. If you still have questions, feel free to fill out the contact form, and if you are with the media and would like to speak or include information in an upcoming story, please additionally send your request to press@mars-one.com.
